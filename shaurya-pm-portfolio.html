0-to-1 Feature Design
Blinkit: Recipe-to-Cart
Bridging content & commerce in quick grocery delivery
🛒

Problem

Users browsing recipes on YouTube/Instagram must manually identify ingredients and search each one on Blinkit — 8-12 taps per ingredient, cross-app context-switching, and ingredient guesswork. An untapped "planned cooking" segment sits right below the surface.

Market signal
2B+ food views/month on YouTube India
AOV opportunity
5–7 items → 12–18 per recipe order
Target segment
Gen Z planned-cooking shoppers

Feature Design

Core flow: Recipe Discovery tab → Recipe Detail with ingredient-SKU auto-mapping → One-Tap "Add All to Cart" → existing checkout. Real-time stock status shown per ingredient with swaps available.

Power feature: Paste-a-Recipe — user pastes any URL (YouTube, food blog), AI extracts ingredients and maps to Blinkit SKUs, bypassing need to host all recipes internally.

Prioritisation (RICE)

P0 · Score 135
One-tap "Add All" button
P0 · Score 18
Recipe tab + 200 curated recipes
P1 · Score 9.3
Creator partnership program

North Star Metrics

Recipe-originated orders as % of total orders. Supporting: ≥12 items per recipe order, 1.8x AOV vs regular orders, 40%+ recipe-to-cart conversion, 30% repeat usage within 14 days.

📰

Core Problem

The feed's engagement-optimised ranking amplifies sensational content, creates echo chambers, and treats all sources equally regardless of credibility. Notifications feel constant, untargeted, and erode trust by blending editorial with ads.

Growth & AI Strategy
Khan Academy: Closing the Engagement Gap
EdTech, Khanmigo AI adoption, and international B2B expansion
🎓

Product Snapshot

Non-profit EdTech platform — 180M registered users, 120M yearly active learners, 190 countries. Revenue ~$120M (2025) primarily philanthropic, with growing KAD B2B channel at 945K licensed students (13% YoY growth).

Core Problem

Only 11% of KAD-licensed students qualify as Very Active Learners. Khanmigo AI was not proactively sought by students — the product required metacognitive skill users didn't have. Engagement design significantly lags Duolingo.

PM Insight: The Khanmigo Pivot

Rather than a standalone chatbot, Khanmigo is now embedded directly in the practice workflow. Classic "reduce activation energy" — if users won't come to the feature, bring the feature to users. Mirrors Grammarly's inline suggestions pattern.

Three Strategic Recommendations

Rec 1 – Engagement Overhaul: Streak-based daily goals, friend accountability, social features layered on new team games. Target DAU/MAU 15% → 25%, YVAL 11% → 20%.

Rec 2 – Proactive AI Nudges: Detect struggle signals (repeated incorrect answers, long pauses) and surface context-aware scaffolding. Target Khanmigo interaction rate 5% → 30%.

Rec 3 – KAD Lite International: Localised content packages for India, Brazil, sub-Saharan Africa aligned to national curricula, offline-first mobile. Target international YVAL 1.1M → 5M in 3 years.

North Star

Skills Mastered per learner per month — directly measures learning outcomes, not vanity engagement.

Feature Addition · 0-to-1
Playo: Squad Mode
Solving the biggest retention gap — no one consistent to play with
🏸

Problem

Playo's core engagement loop is transactional — book a slot, play, leave. Users who want a consistent group of 4-6 trusted players have no native mechanism to form one. Every session is a fresh matchmaking event. Users manage this on WhatsApp, outside Playo — creating a massive retention leak.

Gap 1
No persistent squad formation
Gap 2
Last-minute cancellations unchecked
Gap 3
Karma loop too weak beyond Day 30

Feature: Squad Mode

A named group of 3-8 members with recurring slot booking, weekly RSVP check-ins, and a Smart Bench of 4 fill-in players. When a core member taps "Out," the bench is automatically alerted and the spot is filled — no WhatsApp scrambling. Attendance Reliability Scores per member add social accountability.

RICE Highlights

P0 · Score 80
RSVP + Auto Bench Fill-in
P0 · Score 45
Recurring Booking + Split Pay
P1 · Score 160
Squad Karma Multiplier (1.5x)

Business Case

A Squad user booking weekly generates 52 bookings/year vs 8-12 for casual users. 15% DAU conversion to Squads → ₹93.6 Cr annualised GMV. Paid Squad Wallet at ₹149/month adds direct subscription revenue. Most importantly: squads create social switching costs that venue-inventory competitors cannot match.

North Star

Sessions per active user per month: ~4 → 7+ for squad users. Supporting: 88%+ session attendance rate, 58%+ 30-day retention for squad vs non-squad cohort.

Competitive Analysis
Swiggy Instamart: Retention Mechanics
Subscription loyalty and repeat-purchase loops in q-commerce
⚡

Context

Swiggy Instamart competes directly with Blinkit and Zepto in India's hyper-competitive quick commerce market. While Blinkit leads on dark store density, Instamart's differentiation lies in its Swiggy One membership bundling food + grocery delivery benefits.

Retention Problem

Q-commerce suffers from high switching intent — users choose the cheapest delivery or fastest ETA at the moment of need. Swiggy's challenge: convert transactional users into habitual Instamart-first shoppers without unsustainable discount dependency.

What's Working

Swiggy One bundling: Combining food and grocery free delivery under one subscription dramatically increases switching cost. Users who use both food + grocery see 2.4x higher monthly order frequency. Smart Basket: Predictive reorder surfacing based on purchase history reduces friction for routine restocking.

Gaps Identified

Gap 1
No pantry state management
Gap 2
Weak social/gifting features
Gap 3
Limited hyperlocal content

Recommendations

Pantry Intelligence: Allow users to log pantry items (or infer from order history), then proactively suggest reorder 3 days before predicted depletion. Targets habitual engagement outside active hunger moments.

Social Grocery Lists: Shared household lists with real-time sync — addresses multi-member household use case and increases weekly session frequency. WhatsApp-native sharing for Indian household dynamics.

North Star

Weekly active orderers as % of Swiggy One subscribers — measures whether the subscription is actually building grocery-first habits.

Monetisation & Engagement
Duolingo: The Streak Economy
How habit loops power a $7B EdTech flywheel
🦉

The Core Loop

Duolingo's DAU/MAU of ~27% (vs industry average ~5%) is built on the most sophisticated habit engine in consumer EdTech. The streak mechanic creates a daily obligation that transcends intrinsic motivation — you don't want to lose the streak, not necessarily learn Spanish.

The Streak Economy

Variable reward: XP boosts, league promotions, and gem rewards create dopamine spikes unpredictably enough to drive compulsive checking. Loss aversion: Streak Freeze purchases (using Gems/Lingots) convert engagement into revenue while solving user anxiety. Hearts system on mobile creates scarcity around mistakes — a bold UX decision that drives Super Duolingo upgrades.

Monetisation Funnel

Free tier
Ads + Hearts (mistake scarcity)
Super Duolingo
~$7/mo: no ads, unlimited hearts
Duolingo Max
~$14/mo: GPT-4 roleplay + explain features

Tensions and Risks

Engagement ≠ Learning: Streaks can be maintained by doing the minimum — tapping through one easy lesson. Duolingo's own research shows low correlation between streak length and actual language proficiency. This creates brand risk as users realise the app didn't actually teach them.

Leaderboard manipulation: Diamond League users often bot-play to maintain ranking, degrading the social feature's authenticity.

What PM-me would do

Introduce a "Proficiency Streak" — track consecutive days where the user completes a session above their current difficulty floor. Harder to game, better correlated with outcomes, and creates a differentiable story vs competitors: "Duolingo streaks that actually mean something."
