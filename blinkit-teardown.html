01 - About the Product

What Blinkit does today

Blinkit (formerly Grofers, acquired by Zomato 2022) is India's leading quick-commerce platform, promising 10-minute grocery delivery through a dark store network across 30+ cities.

Blinkit's core use case is the "forgot an ingredient" moment - impulse restocking mastered. But an adjacent segment goes unserved: the planned-cooking user who watches a recipe on Instagram or YouTube and manually translates it into 8-12 individual Blinkit searches. The business model depends on increasing AOV - a recipe order naturally delivers 2x the items of a standard order, making this strategically critical.

02 - Problem Statement

The content-to-commerce gap

2 billion food views per month on YouTube India. Zero native integration between recipe content and Indian grocery apps.

"I watch the recipe, screenshot the ingredients, switch to Blinkit, search each item one by one, figure out quantities, check stock - it takes 15 minutes just to start cooking."- Target user, Bangalore

Three friction dimensions: context-switching between recipe content and a grocery app, ingredient guesswork on quantities and brand equivalents, and 8-12 taps per ingredient when the entire job should be one tap. Planned cooking is a distinct, higher-value intent than panic restocking - and Blinkit has no product for it.

05 - Pain Points

Where the experience breaks

📱
App-switching friction

Moving between recipe content and Blinkit breaks cooking intent. Every switch is a drop-off risk.

High severity
🧮
Quantity guesswork

Recipe videos rarely specify grams. Users infer quantities and often get it wrong - wrong items added silently.

High severity
🔍
Search repetition

12-18 individual searches for one recipe. No memory between sessions. Same friction every time.

High severity
🚫
Out-of-stock dead ends

No substitute suggestions when an ingredient is unavailable. Users abandon the cart or the whole idea.

Medium severity
🏷️
Brand decision fatigue

Multiple SKUs for "paneer 200g." User evaluates each option - cognitive load that kills cooking momentum.

Medium severity
💸
No upfront cost visibility

No sense of total recipe cost until checkout. Price surprise causes abandonment on multi-ingredient recipes.

Medium severity

07 - Solutions Aligned with Business Goals

What to build and why it matters for the business

Recipe Discovery Tab

A Recipes section on the Blinkit home screen with curated recipe cards, categories by cuisine and cook time, and search integration.

Goal: new use case, DAU engagement
Ingredient-to-SKU Auto-mapping

AI model that maps ingredient text to the best-matching Blinkit SKU with real-time stock status from the user's local dark store.

Goal: reduce cart-build time from 15 min to 1 tap
One-tap Add All to Cart

Single button adds all mapped ingredients. Smart deduplication skips items already in cart. Upfront price estimate shown before confirming.

Goal: AOV uplift from 5-7 items to 12-18
Substitution Engine

When an ingredient is unavailable, suggest the closest substitute with a cooking note to reduce full cart abandonment.

Goal: reduce abandonment on stockout
Paste-a-Recipe

User pastes any recipe URL. AI extracts ingredient list and maps to Blinkit SKUs. No dependency on Blinkit hosting the recipe.

Goal: power user retention, open ecosystem
Creator Partnership Program

Partner with food creators on Instagram and YouTube to deep-link their posts to Blinkit recipe pages, with revenue share on attributed orders.

Goal: low-CAC user acquisition via content

10 - Go-to-Market Strategy

How we launch and grow

Phase 1
Week 1-4
Soft launch with 200 curated recipes

Launch Recipe tab to 10% of users in Bangalore and Mumbai. Editorial team curates 200 recipes across 8 Indian cuisines. Measure recipe-to-cart conversion and AOV. Fix mapping errors before wider rollout.

Phase 2
Week 5-8
Creator partnerships + city-wide rollout

Partner with 50 food creators for deep links from their posts to Blinkit recipe pages. Revenue share on attributed orders. Roll out to all cities. 10% discount on first recipe order to drive trial.

Phase 3
Week 9-12
Paste-a-Recipe + retention loops

Launch URL parser for any recipe on the web. Recipe-driven push notifications ("You saved this - ingredients available now"). Target: 5% of orders being recipe-originated by end of quarter.

North Star: Recipe-originated orders as % of total orders - connects AOV (business goal) to engagement (product goal).

Guardrail metrics: Overall order conversion rate must not drop. Customer satisfaction scores. Ingredient mapping accuracy target: 95%+ correct SKU selection.
