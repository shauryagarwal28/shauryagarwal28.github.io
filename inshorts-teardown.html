Inshorts earns attention by making news frictionless - 60-word summaries, one-swipe navigation, no paywalls. The core loop: open app, swipe through stories, share the interesting ones. The problem is the loop optimises for volume of swipes, not quality of information. That distinction matters more as the news ecosystem gets noisier.

02 - Problem Statement

Where the current system breaks down

The feed ranks stories by engagement signals - swipes, shares, time-on-card. Engagement and information quality are not the same thing.

"I open Inshorts to stay informed, but half the stories are things I'd never share - clickbait headlines, repeated angles, ads disguised as news."- Urban professional, 28, Delhi

Three failure modes of engagement-based ranking: clickbait amplification - provocative headlines rank higher than substantive ones even if the underlying story has low factual density. Echo chambers - personalisation without diversity collapses the feed into the same 3 topics. Notification erosion - blending ads with editorial in Breaking News alerts destroys trust in the notification channel entirely.

The business consequence: users who feel the feed is low-quality reduce sessions, turn off notifications, and eventually churn - exactly the users Inshorts most needs to retain (informed professionals who share stories and drive organic growth).

07 - Solutions Aligned with Business Goals

What to build and why

News Quality Score (NQS)

A multi-dimensional ranking signal replacing raw engagement: Source Credibility (25%) + Editorial Rating (25%) + Factual Density (20%) + Uniqueness (15%) + Timeliness (15%).

Goal: feed users trust and return to
Feed Composition Rules

Enforce a quality floor: 40% Must-Know (NQS above 800) + 35% Personalised Quality + 15% Serendipity + 10% Hyperlocal. No slot can run ads without disclosure.

Goal: diversity, reduced echo chambers
3-Tier Notification System

Breaking (NQS above 950, max 2/day, never ads) + Daily Digest (timed to user's best engagement windows) + Weekly Roundup (serendipity re-engagement).

Goal: reverse notification disable trend
Source Credibility Badge

Visible credibility score on each card sourced from Press Council ratings, fact-check history, and editorial standards. Users can tap to see score breakdown.

Goal: build source trust, reduce exit-to-Google
Category-Level Notification Controls

Users set which categories send digests (Politics on, Sports off). Quiet hours stop all notifications 10 PM - 7 AM by default.

Goal: reduce notification disable rate
Quality Engagement Metrics

Replace swipe-volume with Read Time, Share Rate, Return After Story, and Notification Open Rate as the product's internal success metrics.

Goal: align product incentives with user value

10 - Go-to-Market Strategy

How we roll out the quality system

Phase 1
Week 1-8
Build NQS quietly - no user-visible change

Engineering builds the NQS algorithm and starts scoring all stories in the background. A/B test NQS-ranked feed vs current feed with 5% of users. Measure DAU/MAU, session length, and notification interaction rates. No UI changes until we have data.

Phase 2
Week 9-12
Launch 3-tier notification system

Roll out tiered notifications to all users. Make Breaking News a strict editorial signal - no ads, NQS above 950, max 2 per day. Add category-level controls and quiet hours. Measure notification opt-out rate before and after. This is the fastest trust-builder with the least risk.

Phase 3
Week 13-16
Ship NQS feed + source credibility badges

Roll out NQS-ranked feed to 100% of users. Show quality score on cards for power users (optional toggle). Add source credibility badges. Announce publicly: "Inshorts Quality First" - marketing moment that differentiates from social media feeds.

North Star: DAU/MAU ratio. A quality feed drives habitual usage. Target: 42% to 48%+ within 6 months.

Guardrail: Revenue per session must not drop. Quality-ranked ads in non-Breaking slots should maintain or improve CPMs through higher engagement rates on the ads that do run.
