01 - About the Product

What Swiggy Instamart does today

Swiggy Instamart is India's second-largest q-commerce player, promising 10-minute grocery delivery across 30+ cities, bundled with Swiggy One subscription alongside food delivery.

Competition: Blinkit, Zepto

Instamart's structural advantage is the Swiggy One bundle - a combined food + grocery subscription that Blinkit and Zepto can't easily replicate. The challenge is converting that structural advantage into a grocery-first habit. Currently, most Instamart orders are reactive (ran out of something) rather than planned. The habitual weekly grocery shopper who anchors their routine to Instamart is the user worth winning - and they're currently switching between all three platforms based on offer availability.

02 - Problem Statement

The habit-formation gap in q-commerce

In a market where Blinkit, Instamart, and Zepto all deliver in 10 minutes, retention comes from switching costs - not product speed.

"I have Swiggy One but I still open Blinkit when I need groceries because they show me a better offer. The subscription doesn't actually make me loyal."- Swiggy One subscriber, 31, Mumbai

The core problem: Swiggy One creates a financial commitment but not a behavioural habit. The subscription reduces the cost of each order but doesn't change when or why the user orders. Without predicting the user's need before they feel it, Instamart is always competing on the moment of intent - when all three apps are equally available and equally fast. The window to win is before the need is felt.

07 - Solutions Aligned with Business Goals

What to build and why

Pantry Depletion Model

Backend system that infers consumption rates from order history. Predicts when each frequently-ordered item is ~3 days from depletion and surfaces a proactive reorder prompt.

Goal: reach user before they open a competitor
Proactive Reorder Notification

"Your cooking oil might be running low - you usually reorder every 3 weeks." One-tap to open a pre-filled restock cart. Timed to 3 days before predicted depletion.

Goal: convert reactive to proactive habit
Shared Household List

Multiple household members add to the same Instamart list in real-time. One member checks out for everything. WhatsApp-native sharing generates a link anyone can add items to.

Goal: replace WhatsApp coordination, build stickiness
Pantry View

Visual dashboard showing estimated stock levels for frequently-bought items. Depletion bars inferred from order history. Low items surface a one-tap reorder. Gets smarter with more orders.

Goal: make Instamart the household's pantry manager
Recurring Staples Basket

Household sets a "Staples List" - items that auto-populate a new cart every Monday. One-tap to confirm and order. Replaces the kirana shopkeeper's memory of what your household always needs.

Goal: habit formation, weekly GMV anchor
Swiggy One Grocery Perks

Exclusive Instamart perks for Swiggy One subscribers: priority dark store allocation at peak times, exclusive weekly bundle price, free delivery on all grocery orders regardless of cart value.

Goal: make subscription tangibly valuable for grocery

10 - Go-to-Market Strategy

How we launch Pantry Intelligence

Phase 1
Week 1-6
Build depletion model silently

Data science team trains depletion prediction model using 12 months of order history. No user-visible changes. Validate model accuracy internally - target 80%+ precision on predicted reorder date. Only launch notifications when model is confident enough to avoid false predictions.

Phase 2
Week 7-10
Launch proactive notifications to Swiggy One subscribers

Start with Swiggy One users only - they're the highest LTV cohort and the subscription makes the "we know your patterns" framing feel earned rather than creepy. A/B test notification copy: predictive ("running low") vs factual ("you last ordered oil 18 days ago"). Measure open rate and conversion to order.

Phase 3
Week 11-16
Pantry view + household list + rollout

Launch pantry view and shared household list together - they reinforce each other. Roll out pantry intelligence to all users (not just Swiggy One). Use pantry adoption as a Swiggy One upgrade driver: "Unlock recurring smart restocks with Swiggy One."

North Star: Weekly active orderers as % of Swiggy One subscribers. Measures whether the subscription is building grocery-first habits, not just one-off convenience orders.

Guardrail: Notification disable rate must not increase. If proactive notifications feel intrusive, users disable the channel entirely - worse than before. Precision over recall: fewer, more accurate predictions beat frequent guesses.
